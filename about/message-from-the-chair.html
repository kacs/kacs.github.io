Message from the KACS Chair

I am honored to be the 2020 Chair of the Kalamazoo Local Section of the American Chemical Society. I have greatly enjoyed meeting many of you at KACS events the last couple of years, including the Sustainable Science – Recycle a Poster event, Chemistry Day at the Museum, Kalamazoo National Historic Landmark Celebration, 46th annual Awards Gathering, Speed Networking event, and others. These events would not be possible without the volunteers within our section who generously donate their time to make them such a success. The most important thing I can say is THANK YOU to all the volunteers, who for more than three quarters of a century has made the KACS a very successful local section.

The 2nd most important thing I can do is to ask for current members to help keep this tradition going! In my opinion our educational outreach is the most vital thing we do as a section, and for the last few years we have NOT had any members regularly serving on our education committee. I attended our 46th annual awards gathering on May 6th, 2019. Awards were presented to outstanding graduating seniors from WMU and Kalamazoo College, a high school chemistry teacher highly thought of by his students, a distinguished KACS Volunteer and to a 60-year ACS member. In addition, certificates and prizes (monetary) were presented to outstanding high school chemistry students as determined by their scores on a 2-tiered examination. Unfortunately, without an education committee this popular program is at a very real risk of disappearing. We are looking for a team willing to help the KACS executive committee keep this tradition alive. If you have an interest in education or simply want to see the smiling faces of young people not looking at a screen, then I implore you to email me and discuss this wonderful opportunity.

Finally, if anyone would like to discuss the KACS, and/or programs we can implement that would be of interest to the chemical community, or anything else, then reach out to me or come to our monthly executive committee meeting which is usually held at 6:30 pm on the third Tuesday of each month. I wish everyone a very successful 2020 and look forward to hearing from you,

Hershel Jude
KACS Chair

Hershel Jude

Message from the KACS Chair-Elect

I am pleased to serve as the 2020 Chair Elect and 2021 Chair of the Kalamazoo Section of the American Chemical Society. I have over 35 years of pharmaceutical research and development experience and am a member of ACS for 47 years.

With all the benefits I have received from the ACS, I decided it was time to give back. In the past I given demonstrations at Chemistry Day and visited local elementary schools to share with them the excitement of science and chemistry. Last year I was super charged by our local section receiving the prestigious ACS National Historic Chemistry Landmark Award. I especially enjoyed the chemistry symposium on the day of the award, May 17, 2019, and the social event and dinner the previous evening. I wish to continue on the local committee to implement interesting scientific member meetings.

Membership enjoyment and participation, scientific meetings and education are my focus for this year. I hope you share with me or other executive members your interests and needs. Please feel free to contact me.

I look forward to helping the Kalamazoo ACS section achieve Hershel Jude's and our local section's goals through successful activities and increased membership participation.

Edward W. Thomas
KACS Chair-Elect
