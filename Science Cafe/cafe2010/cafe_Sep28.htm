The Kalamazoo Section of the American Chemical Society held a Science Café event upstairs at Martini's Pizza on 28 September 2010.  It featured Dr. Michael Barcelona, a chemist at Western Michigan University, who led a discussion of “What Lies (and moves) Beneath? Ground Water Quality Issues.”

Dr. Barcelona’s discussion centered around some current groundwater contamination cases, including Los Alamos National Laboratory in New Mexico and the Enbridge pipeline spill in the Kalamazoo River and its cleanup.  About 25 people, including three children, participated in the presentation and discussion.
