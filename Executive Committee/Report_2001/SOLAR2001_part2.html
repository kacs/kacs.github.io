Activity #1
a) Title:
Joint 33rd C/GL Regional Meeting
b) Description
(please limit to
one paragraph):		 
Our section served as co-host, along with the Western 
Michigan Section, for the Joint 33rd Central/33rd 
Great Lakes Regional Meeting.  The meeting, 
titled “2001: A Grand Chemical Odyssey,” was held June 
11-13 at the Amway Grand Plaza Hotel in Grand Rapids, 
MI.  The main contributors from our section were Brian 
Stockman (General Co-Chair), Dawn Merritt (Program Co-
Chair), John Miller (Exhibitions Co-Chair), Don 
Schreiber (Solicitations Co-Chair) and Ben Maxey 
(Publicity Co-Chair).  The meeting was a scientific 
and financial success.  The total number of attendees 
was approximately 800.  A total of 5 workshops and 478 
abstracts were presented in 67 invited sessions and 
two poster sessions.  For more information refer to 
http://membership.acs.org/W/WMI/2001/.

Activity #2
a) Title
Awards Banquet: HS, College, Section
b) Description
(Please limit
to one paragraph)		 
Our Awards Banquet organized by Dean Cooke was held on 
May 21 on the campus of Western Michigan University in 
the Bernhard Center’s West Ballroom.  Approximately 
100 were in attendance.  This occasion provides an 
excellent opportunity to bring together high school 
students, their teachers and parents, college 
students, and other members of our section.  For the 
28th year, we recognized an Outstanding High School 
Chemistry Teacher and Outstanding Senior Student in 
Chemistry.  The students each received a copy of the 
Handbook of Chemistry and Physics.  For the 20th year, 
we organized and sponsored the High School Competitive 
Scholarships in Chemistry.  Prizes, ranging from $25 
to $1,000, were presented to the winners at the 
banquet.  In addition, monetary prizes for Outstanding 
College Chemistry Students were awarded.  Fifty-Year 
Members and Section Awardees were also recognized at 
this gala event.  A copy of a related Kalamazoo 
Gazette article is attached as awards.pdf.

Activity #3
a) Title
Project SEED
b) Description
Please limit
to one paragraph		 
With financial support from local industries and the 
National ACS, our section sponsored four students in 
Project SEED.  Our program, coordinated by Don 
Schreiber, provided hands-on research opportunities on 
the following topics:  “Investigation of Variability 
in the Wet Chemistry Analysis of Peppermint and 
Spearmint Oils” (AM Todd Company), “The Effect of 
Polychlorinated Biphenyls on the Cloning of Human 
Fibroblasts” (Western Michigan 
University), “Nanotechnology: Applications and 
Preparations Of Quantum Dot Systems” (Western Michigan 
University), and “Working with Labview” (Western 
Michigan University).  Students (and their families) 
presented their projects and met with other section 
members during a poster display and reception on 
August 2.  One student presented a poster describing 
their project at the Chicago National ACS Meeting.

Activity #4
a) Title:
National Chemistry Week
b) Description
(Please limit
to one paragraph)		 
Under the direction of Lydia Hines, programming during 
and around National Chemistry Week continued to be one 
of our priorities.  Activities included a chemistry 
career day at Western Michigan University attended by 
350 high school students and featuring the keynote 
address, “The Chemistry of Stained Glass”; 
distribution of 1,000 copies of “Celebrating Chemistry 
and Art” and 350 copies of “ChemMatters”; adornment of 
a major Kalamazoo overpass with our banner, “Chemistry-
Solutions for the Future”; Chemistry Day at the 
Kalamazoo Valley Museum, a hands-on experience 
featuring about 20 different activities that was 
attended by 1,200 patrons; and events by our student 
affiliate groups including the Chemistry Spectacular 
(WMU) and a Sistine Chapel restoration workshop 
(Kalamazoo College).  A copy of a related Kalamazoo 
Gazette article is attached as museum.pdf.

Spring ACS tour speaker.  Dr. Bruce B. Jarvis from the 
Department of Chemistry and Biochemistry at the 
University of Maryland presented a seminar titled “Are 
Secondary Metabolites Really Secondary?” on February 
20.  During his visit, he also discussed his thoughts 
on science with the combined junior/senior classes at 
the Kalamazoo Area Math & Science Center.  Including 
his seminars, discussions with media and faculty 
members, and dinner with section members, a total of 
~200 individuals interacted with Dr. Jarvis.  A copy 
of a related Kalamazoo Gazette article is attached as 
spring_tour.pdf.

Fall ACS tour speaker.  Dr. David F. Wiemer from the 
Department of Chemistry at the University of Iowa 
presented a seminar titled, “A Search for Natural 
Insecticides” on October 16.  During his visit, he 
also gave a special version of his seminar to the 
combined junior/senior classes at the Kalamazoo Area 
Math & Science Center.  Including his seminars, 
discussions with media and faculty members, and dinner 
with section members, a total of ~160 individuals 
interacted with Dr. Weimer.  A copy of a related 
Kalamazoo Gazette article is attached as fall_tour.pdf.

Kalchemist News.  Paul Dobrowolski took over as Editor 
of our Kalchemist News newsletter.  Under Paul’s 
energetic leadership, the Kalchemist News is now 
issuing in a timely fashion following a lapse of about 
six months.  We began exploring mechanisms for 
electronic distribution of this important 
communication vehicle.  A copy of the Nov/Dec 2001 
Kalchemist News is located at 
http://www.wmich.edu/acs/kalchemnews/KalchemistNov01.pd
f.

Continuing Education Support.  A new budget category

for members.  Funds were used to partially support 
attendance at the San Diego National ACS Meeting by
