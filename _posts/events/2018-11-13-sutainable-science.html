Digging for Gold in Your Own Backyard: Novel Enzymes for Use in Flavors & Fragrances

by Katie Whalen, Ph.D.
Principal Scientist and Team Lead
Biotechnology & Food Protection R&D at Kalsec, Inc.

Funding provided through a generous grant by

As of September 2018, there are 124 million enzyme sequences deposited in public databases. Is there one to do the chemical reaction you want? Katie will discuss the process of discovering novel enzyme "needles" in a haystack that grows by 5 million every month. Applications related to natural manufacturing of flavor and fragrance molecules will be discussed, including the semi-serendipitous discovery of a novel route to the pheromone, sulcatone.

Current ACS members, poster presenters and Zoetis employees are eligible for two complimentary drink tickets.
