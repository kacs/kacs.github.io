Answer to the April Puzzler:

"Who invented the rotary evaporator?"

Lyman Craig (1906-1974)

This was a tough one! |While I didn't receive a correct answer, but I do extend my thanks to Dave Anderson and Paul Aristoff for their educated guesses. While the name reflexively associated with the rotary evaporator is that of its Swiss manufacturer, it is indeed an American invention made by a true chemical inventor, Lyman Craig, in the early 1940's while at the Rockefeller University. Craig's awards in chemistry included the Lasker Award, the Kolthoff medal, and membership in the National Academy. The all-too-brief obituary published by C&E News (22 Jul 1974) acknowledges Craig for his invention of the glass countercurrent distribution apparatus (an invention now firmly supplanted by technology) without mention of his one simple yet outstanding invention that still spins, even today, in thousands of laboratories around the world. Thanks once again, Lyman.
