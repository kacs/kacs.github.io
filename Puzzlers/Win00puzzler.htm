Answer to the Winter 2000 Puzzler

"In its early youth, organic chemistry was so closely connected to biology. I do consider it not only possible, but desirable, that the close connection of chemistry and biology .... should be reestablished, as the great chemical secrets of life are only to be unveiled by cooperative work."

-Emil Fischer (taken from the Faraday Lecture, published J. Chem. Soc. 1907, 1749.
(Quoted by Waldmann et al. ChemBiochem 2000, 1, 144)

"It is the discipline of science that enables all of us ordinary people, whether we be chemists or biologists, to go about doing the ordinary things, which when assembled, reveal the extraordinary intricacies and awesome beauties of nature"

-Arthur Kornberg, taken from his essay "Centenary of the Birth of Modern Biochemistry"
FASEB J. 1997, 11, 1209

Kornberg is my selection as the scientist to succeed Fischer as the bio-chemical visionary for the start of this new century. Kornberg is among the biochemical greats of the twentieth century, whose intolerance for experiments with "dirty" reagents is justifiably famous. He has written several essays on the topic of chemistry&endash;biochemistry&endash;biology synergism over the past dozen years (the citation above is the most recent). While his understanding of modern organic chemistry is simplistic (as will be evident immediately to the bio-organic or medicinal chemist who ventures into his writings), the certitude of his conviction that genomics/proteomics are self-limiting with respect to the extraordinary intricacies, emerges unchallenged. The profound revelations of Nature for the future will be made by extraordinary teams of ordinary chemists, biologists, engineers, physicists ...... all working together, as an integrated ensemble of discoverers.
